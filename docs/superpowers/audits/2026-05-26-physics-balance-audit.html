§12

Literal physics — thermodynamics, drag, optics, energy conservation

User clarified "physics" means actual physics, not simulation balance. Findings below: what the implementation does vs what real physics expects, and where the gap is acknowledged in code comments vs silently fudged.

12.1 Mass & energy conservation in the integrator

The §15.3 piecewise-constant-rate integrator (economy.ts) is mass-conserving by design: per-segment rates are capped by inputAvail (input shortage) and outputAvail (cap headroom), and the next-event finder enumerates depletion + saturation crossings before each segment. verified A 24-hour offline catchup reuses the same loop as a 16 ms frame — deterministic and identical results.

Within recipes, mass conservation is NOT enforced — the catalog declares whatever input/output ratios it wants (see §13 for stoichiometry violations). The integrator faithfully executes whatever the catalog says.

12.2 Heat is unmetered N:1 adjacency — free energy by design

verified heat.ts:1-7 says it explicitly: "A single source serves any number of adjacent consumers; the source's fuel consumption (if any) multiplies by the count of consumers it serves." Plasma Heater (200 W consumer, free heat source) can serve 1, 4, or 40 adjacent Coke Ovens (each needing 60 W of "heat") at zero incremental cost. Each consumer just needs an adjacent source — no thermal flux accounting.

Physically: energy conservation violated. A 200 W heater radiating into N consumers needing 60 W each cannot supply more than 200/60 ≈ 3 consumers at thermodynamic break-even. The game allows unlimited. Design intent: heat is a gameplay GATE (adjacency check), not a resource — comments make this explicit. Players reasoning in joules will notice; reasoning in "is there a furnace next to my forge" works.

12.5 Solar curve — 50% capacity factor, real is 15–25%

verified Solar curve is dawn 0→1 linear over 6 h, day 1.0 over 6 h, dusk 1→0 linear over 6 h, night 0 over 6 h. Integral over 24 h = 0.5 × 6 + 1.0 × 6 + 0.5 × 6 + 0 × 6 = 12 effective full-power hours of 24 = 50% capacity factor. Real solar: best US sites ~25%, US average ~15%. Game over-produces by 2-3× vs reality.

Defensible as gameplay simplification ("idle-game-friendly solar"), but labels the player's solar arrays as systematically richer than real arrays of the same nameplate W.

12.6 Signal / vision propagation — simple circles, no inverse-square

verified Lighthouse vision and Antenna signal ranges are simple discs (Euclidean point-in-circle test). No inverse-square attenuation, no line-of-sight, no atmospheric/oceanic loss. Lighthouse radii T1-T6 = 50 / 80 / 120 / 160 / 220 / 300 tiles; antenna radii T1-T6 = 80 / 140 / 220 / 320 / 480 / 700 tiles. Sonar buoy = 4 cells = 64 tiles world-space. lighthouse.ts:37-44; antenna.ts:23-29; sonar-buoy.ts:38 Acceptable gameplay simplification.

12.7 Orbital mechanics — handwaved, not modeled

Sat fuel = 0.05 × distance(tiles), uniform regardless of sat mass (real Δv ∝ ln(mass_ratio)). Launch success T1/T2/T3+ = 30/50/70 % capped at 99 % (real modern launches > 95 %). Mirror Sat Lorentzian peak boost 0.7 at r_half = 200 t (locked per spec; real optics would be multiplicative not additive). Debris hit constant 0.0005 / tile (intentionally deadly for orbital drama). All explicitly placeholder / gameplay-tuned, not physics claims. verified

13.4 Refining & cryogenics

  • naphtha_cracker 1 crude_oil → 1 naphtha. Real cracking yields a distribution of products. Game collapses to one output per building (separate diesel_refinery / kerosene_refinery for other fractions). verified recipes.ts:2440
  • crude_oil_cracker 3 crude_oil → 1 heavy_oil + 1 tar + 1 asphalt. 3:3 mass conserving; the actual residue/loss not modeled.
  • lubricant_refinery 1 crude_oil + 1 chlorine → 1 lubricant. Chlorine is not a real lubricant additive — real lube uses VI improvers, detergents, antioxidants. verified recipes.ts:2528-2536 Fantasy chemistry.
  • kerosene_refinery 3 crude_oil + 1 hydrogen → 1 aviation_kerosene. Hydrogen hydrotreatment is real — sensible chemistry. verified
  • cryogenic_generator 1 cryo_coolant → power. verified "Burns" cryo coolant as fuel; real cryo expansion uses Brayton/Joule-Thomson cycles, not combustion. Wrong physics for the named mechanism, but functional as a fueled power source.
  • cryo_lab 1 hydrogen + 1 nitrogen → 1 cryo_coolant. No real chemistry basis — cryo coolants are typically pure LN₂, LHe, or LH₂. Fantasy blend.
  • cryo_compressor 1 hydrogen + 1 cryo_coolant → 1 cryogenic_hydrogen. Real liquefaction via Linde cycle is sensible; the cryo_coolant as a reagent is the unrealistic part.

13.5 Acknowledged fantasy chemistry

Spec is explicit that these don't claim chemistry: exotic_alloy (5 steel + 1 helium_3, lines 1530-1534 — helium is noble, doesn't alloy), quantum_chip, ai_core, antimatter_capsule, time_crystal, casimir_energy, reality_anchor, memetic_core, eldritch_processor, phase_converter, zero_point_flux. Carbon Fiber from wood + coke (carbon_forge 5 wood + 2 coke → 1 carbon_fiber) is similarly fantasy — real CF comes from PAN polymer. All explicitly endgame sci-fi; no rebuke owed.

13.6 Environmental chemistry — absent

No CO₂ accumulation (despite limekiln + coke + coal_gen all emitting in real life), no acid rain, no runoff, no thermal pollution downstream of cooling_tower. The only environmental loop is the reactor_toxicity gate (5 %/hour roll when reactors are adjacent) and the wastewater_treatment soft-gate. Suggestion is design-level, not bug: a CO₂ pool tracked as a "free" resource that triggers weather penalties when high would close the loop without much engine work.

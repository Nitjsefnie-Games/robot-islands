Coal loop self-sustain — can coal_mine + coal_gen close on its own?

Question Does the mine (placed on a coal tile, recipe mine_on_coal) produce enough coal — net of the coal_gen's own consumption — to keep one coal generator fed, while the generator's power output covers the mine's draw? If 1:1 doesn't close, what ratio does? Reference methodology: the Heavy Logger / Biomass Plant rebalance (9cda2aa) — a deliberately tuned self-sustaining T2 forestry power loop.

Verdict — NOT self-sustaining at any ratio
No combination of mines + coal_gens closes both balances. Coal-balance needs ≥ 8.5 mines per generator; that ratio guarantees a ≥ 240 W power deficit per generator.
A single mine_on_coal outputs 1 coal / 17 s = 0.0588 coal/s; a single coal_gen burns 1 coal / 2 s = 0.5 coal/s. The coal pipe alone demands N ≥ 8.5 · M (mines : generators). Substituting that floor into the power equation 100·M − 40·N gives 100·M − 340·M = −240·M W — every generator that's actually fed costs more power than it produces, by a wide margin.
In-game adjacency buffs (same_def +10%/match, max 2 → max +20%) and skill-tree mining bonuses don't close the gap: to flip the power sign you'd need N ≤ 2.5·M, i.e. a 3.4× (+240%) mine output multiplier. The largest stock buff (+20%) is ~17× too small. The chain is structurally a power sink, not a power source.

Why no ratio closes

Let N = mines, M = generators. The two balance equations must both be non-negative for self-sustain.

coal balance: N · (1/17) − M · (1/2) ≥ 0 ⇒ N ≥ 8.5 · M power balance: M · 100 − N · 40 ≥ 0 ⇒ N ≤ 2.5 · M

The two constraints are mutually exclusive: N ≥ 8.5·M and N ≤ 2.5·M have no positive-integer solution (no real solution either). Plugging the coal-floor N = 8.5·M into the power equation:

power_net(N = 8.5·M) = 100·M − 40 · 8.5·M = 100·M − 340·M = −240 · M W

Every generator that is actually fed enough coal costs 240 W of net power. Scaling the chain only deepens the hole.

Closure-multiplier needed on mine output. To make both inequalities consistent, mine output must rise by at least 8.5 / 2.5 = 3.4× (a +240% buff). The strongest in-game multiplier on a single mine from its adjacencyBuffs (same-def, +10%/match, maxMatches: 2) is +20%. Skill-tree mineYieldBonusMul nodes add further percentage stacks, but reaching +240% from baseline would require deep tree investment that the audit's "intrinsic balance" framing doesn't grant.

The +60 W is illusory. Once coal drains to zero, the generator's input-availability falls to zero and the recipe stops; computeRates pass-2 multiplies the recipe rate by inputAvail, so power.produces is still 100 W in spec but the recipe-bound running time goes to zero. After the single-mine production-rate steady-state kicks in (gen running at 1/8.5 of nominal duty cycle), effective gen output ≈ 100 · 0.0588 / 0.5 = +11.76 W, and effective coal drain ≈ 0. Power net @ steady state ≈ 11.76 − 40 = −28.2 W. The chain is a net power consumer even when the generator is mostly idle waiting for coal.

Self-sustain? No (at any ratio) Yes (at 1:1, with headroom) —

The two loops were not tuned to comparable economy. The forestry-loop rebalance picked numbers that close (9 wood / 10 s against 1 wood / 2 s burn) — extractor-rate exceeds burn even at 1:1. The coal loop's extractor (1 / 17 s) was sized for the smelter and workshop pipeline that consumes coal at a much slower pace (1 / 27 s and 1 / 33 s), not for a 2-second-cycle generator.

Mismatch root cause. The coal_gen.cycleSec of 2 s is documented in recipes.ts as "power buildings stay at original scale" (step #19 rebalance preserved short cycles so power keeps up with consumers). That logic is sound for power keeping pace with downstream demand, but it means the generator's fuel intake rate is also at the un-rebalanced fast cadence, while the mine sits at the 10× stretched extraction cadence (5 s → 50 s → ÷3 = 17 s). The result: gen burns coal 8.5× faster than one mine can dig it.

Caveats & what the audit excluded

CaveatEffect on conclusion
Adjacency buff on mine (same_def +10%/match, max 2) Max +20% mine throughput → ratio floor improves from 8.5 to ~7.08, power deficit shrinks from −240 W to ~−183 W per generator. Still negative.
Skill-tree mineYieldBonusMul (mining sub-path) Stacks multiplicatively per node; closing the gap requires +240% on top of baseline. Spec doesn't claim the loop closes at L0 — but the audit's framing is "intrinsic baseline", so skilltree boost is out of scope. Even with skill investment, the user needs to know that without it the chain is unviable.
Skill-tree recipeRateMul · extraction Multiplies the mine recipe globally; same comment as above — requires deep tree investment.
Day/night cycle coal_gen is not solar; mine_on_coal is not solar. Day/night has no effect on either side, so the loop's deficit is constant 24/7.
Maintenance & toxicity factors Both apply multiplicative reductions to the mine's output rate but never increase it. Including them only widens the deficit; excluding them gives the loop its best case.
Other coal consumers (smelter, workshop) Out of scope — the question is the closed mine+generator loop. In practice the mine's 1/17 s output is sized for smelter/workshop, both of which consume coal at 1/27 s and 1/33 s respectively. One mine can feed multiple smelter+workshop pairs, but cannot feed a single coal generator.
Third-resource leakage Neither mine_on_coal nor coal_gen takes any input outside the closed loop (inputs: {} and { coal: 1 }). The system is genuinely closed apart from power; no hidden dependency breaks the assumption.

If a rebalance is desired, the cleanest knob is coal_gen.cycleSec: 2 → ≥ 17/(2·M_per_N) aligned with how much coal one mine actually delivers (e.g. cycleSec: 17 would make a 1:1 chain coal-balanced but the power balance would still be tight at +60 W). Alternatively bump mine_on_coal.outputs.coal from 1 to ≥ 9 per cycle — same shape as the Heavy Logger rebalance. Either change is one line in recipes.ts; the audit takes no implementation action.

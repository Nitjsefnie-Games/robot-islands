Lattice shared-flow: one flow problem per component, cap-proportional distribution

Context Closes bug-sweep deferred item D-01 — Lattice unified pool never drains partner islands (matter from nothing). With the Omniscient Lattice active, eligibility reads the pooled inventory but consumption decrements only local stock (clamped at 0): a member island smelts forever off a partner's ore that never shrinks. Also the value-fabrication class the server-authoritative migration exists to prevent. Design locked by the owner in-session.
Decision (owner, 2026-06-10)
Lattice members share the net-flow solver and a pooled inventory, redistributed per-island proportionally to caps.
Reuses the 2026-06-10 net-flow machinery instead of patching around it: the member islands' buildings form one solver problem with regimes from pooled inventory vs pooled caps; after each segment the pooled stock is written back as local_i = pooled × cap_i / Σcaps. Mass conserved by construction — no drain pass, no schema bump, clean deactivation.
01

Mechanics

  1. Union solve. Pass 2.5 feeds the solver the union of member islands' flow coefficients; cap/zero regimes computed from Σ member inventories vs Σ member caps per resource. Cross-island producers/consumers throttle against each other with the exact same shared-θ/φ + min-rule semantics as same-island flows. The solver itself needs no changes (building-set agnostic).
  2. Cap-proportional distribution. After each segment: local_i = pooled × cap_i / Σcaps (resources with Σcaps = 0 keep local stocks untouched). Free properties: every slice fits its island's cap; persistence unchanged (per-island inventories are simply the distributed representation); deactivation clean (islands keep their current shares).
  3. Lockstep advance. Members integrate as a group: one segment timeline, findNextCapEvent over pooled inventories plus each member's own maintenance/construction boundaries (min across members). Non-members advance per-island exactly as today. Offline catchup with Lattice active MUST use the grouped path (high-risk if missed — explicit test).
  4. Per-island attribution unchanged. XP on the producing island; wear and maintenance per-building; power stays on the pass-3 cable-component model — the philosophical precedent this design extends to resources.
02

Touch points

  • economy.ts: pass-2.5 union assembly + pooled regimes; pooled accounting + distribution at applyRates level; pooled view in findNextCapEvent; DerivationsMemo stays per-island (cross-island gates already in its signature via ctx.crossIsland).
  • main.ts advanceEconomy + offline catchup: grouped advance for the lattice component, gated on latticeActive; non-lattice path byte-identical.
  • SPEC.md §13.3: pooled-flow + cap-proportional-distribution semantics (conservation).
03

Tests (minimum)

  • D-01 regression: consumer on A (zero local stock) + producer stock on B → pool drains, B's share shrinks, total mass conserved to 1e-9 over a long advance.
  • Distribution: redistribution by cap share; cap-0 island holds 0 (unless Σcaps=0 freeze).
  • Cross-island throttle θ equals the colocated same-island case (solver-union equivalence anchor).
  • Deactivation mid-state: shares freeze, per-island advance resumes; mass-balance + offline≡online invariants hold.

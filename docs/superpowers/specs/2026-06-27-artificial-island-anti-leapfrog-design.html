Artificial-island anti-leapfrog: make reaching a merge cost real investment

Context Island merging (§3.6) is intentionally a side effect of geometry — when two inscribed-tile footprints touch, they merge. That is fine. What is broken is how cheaply a player can manufacture those touches: by leapfrogging artificial islands across open ocean, a whole archipelago (and the entire logistics pillar built around it) collapses into one self-sufficient megatile for a few thousand units of concrete. This spec gates artificial-island construction with three placement-time rules; it does not change the merge trigger and never un-merges existing saves.

Keep merge-as-side-effect; gate artificial construction with anchor + range + ratio rules.
All three are pure predicates in construction-gate.ts, re-run identically by the construction UI, the LOCAL gateway, and the authoritative construct-island server intent — the established trust-surface pattern. They attack the cheap half of the leapfrog (an existing island growing one step to swallow a pre-positioned platform) and bound reach + count.
Deliberately not changing the merge trigger, not touching the flat LAND_TILE_COST basket (a separate optional cost-curve change), and not retroactively splitting any merged island — merge stays permanent.
01

The exploit (observed on migtest)

Verified against the live Postgres save, not assumed.

home is one merged island of 17 constituents (base + 16 absorbed ellipses), 10 of them artificial — two artificial islands (art-1, art-2) each reclamation-grown to biome max, reaching out to offsetX −285. The account has routes / drones / vehicles / satellites = 0 despite fully unlocking every transport / submarine / network keystone — proof the logistics game was deleted, not played.

The player's own technique:

place an artificial island so it merges when the main island's edge grows to it
→ grow the merged island to max−1
→ drop a new artificial island next to it
→ one reclamation step merges it
→ repeat

Each hop is one placement plus a single reclamation tile-step by the already-existing island. It defeats BIOME_MAX_RADII as a reach limit, because each new artificial lobe is a fresh r≤28 budget — unbounded reach at ~one tile-ring per hop.

02

Verified current behavior vs. the gated state

What the code does today, against what these rules change.

Today (exploitable)
  • LAND_TILE_COST = { steel_beam:1, concrete:10 } per tile — flat, no size/constituent scaling.
  • Server construct-island re-checks overlap + visibility only — no range, no anti-leapfrog gate.
  • Place in clear visible water, grow into contact — overlap passes at placement, merge lands one step later.
  • One founder can spawn unlimited artificial islands.
After (gated)
  • Anchor: cannot place within any populated island's max-growth footprint — the new island must grow itself in.
  • Range: must be within ARTIFICIAL_RANGE_TILES (48) of the founder.
  • Ratio: a founder's lifetime artificial builds ≤ 2× its natural constituents.
  • Merge trigger, existing saves, and cost basket untouched.
03

The three rules

All evaluated at artificial-island construction time, footprint-based to match the existing overlap test.

RulePredicateEffect
1 · Anchor Reject if the candidate footprint touches/overlaps the max-growth footprint of any populated island — every constituent rasterized at its BIOME_MAX_RADII (hub-independent). New helper maxGrowthFootprintTouches() over the existing islandsOverlap machinery. Forces a gap no existing island can close → the new island must grow itself in. Kills the "cheap one-step swallow". Strict margin (touch = reject), tunable.
2 · Range Reject if the candidate footprint's nearest-tile distance to the founder island exceeds ARTIFICIAL_RANGE_TILES = 48 (placeholder, tunable). No giant leaps / distant land-grabs. With Rule 1 the legal zone is a band: beyond every max-growth reach, yet ≤48 tiles from the founder.
3 · Ratio Reject if attributedArtificial(founder) + 1 > 2 × naturalConstituents(founder). attributedArtificial counts every artificial origin anywhere (standalone + absorbed lobes) with founderId === founder.id; an artificial founder has 0 natural → cannot build. Capacity to manufacture artificial land is proportional to natural mass; absorbing natural islands raises the ceiling, but artificial land can't bootstrap itself. Lifetime count — merging never resets it.

05

Verification

Rule 1. The exact leapfrog placement (touches max-growth but not current footprint) is rejected; just beyond max-growth is accepted.
Rule 2. >48 tiles from founder rejected; ≤48 accepted; measured footprint-to-footprint, not centre-to-centre.
Rule 3. Founder with N natural accepts up to 2N builds, rejects 2N+1; a previously-merged artificial island still counts; artificial founder (0 natural) rejected outright.
Migration. v32 fixture with an artificial lobe loads to v33 with founderId backfilled; v33 round-trips identity; a migtest-shaped save stays legal.
Parity. LOCAL and REMOTE reject identically for each reason (crafted-intent test).

07

Out of scope

Considered, deliberately deferred.

Cost-curve (LAND_TILE_COST) scaling
The flat concrete×10 per tile is a real secondary lever (each hop is still cheap in bulk goods), but the three structural rules are what break the leapfrog. A super-linear size/constituent cost curve can follow as a separate change if still desired after these land.

Design spec · robot-islands · energy SI rebalance

Convert electrical power to real SI units (kW)

Context The SI rework defined "1 unit = 100 kW" but never applied it. Power values are raw abstract numbers, the HUD labels them "W" (hud.ts:577), and the battery table is sized in real kWh — three inconsistent halves. Research showed the consumers and small renewables are already kW-correct; the fix is the unit label + the big generators + two electric-process furnaces + the battery/HUD. Full per-building data: 2026-05-29-energy-si-consumer-table.md.
Locked decisions
  1. Canonical unit = kW (consistent with thermalKW + battery kWh; kW × h = kWh). Display auto-formats W / kW / MW / GW via fmtPower(kW).
  2. Accept the realistic generation cliff — true-SI generators (coal_gen 5 MW…); re-tune scarcity via cost / coal supply / recipe rates, not by shrinking generators.
  3. Drop requiresHeat on the Electric Arc Furnace — it is its own electric heat → pure-electric 10 MW.
  4. consumes is electrical-only — heat-fed furnaces keep small aux loads; smelting heat flows through thermalKW (no double-count).

04

Progression impact — power tension by stage

Build-cost gates (not just kW) shape the curve. Early generators from basic materials are only water_wheel (20 kW, ~4 on home) and windmill (15 kW); solar is T3-gated, geothermal_vent (1 MW) is volcanic-only, and coal_gen/biomass/wind_turbine need steel_beam — unavailable until the steel chain, which itself needs power. Result: a sawtooth.

StageTier/levelGenerationKey loadsTension
Early bootstrapT0–T1, pre-steelwater_wheel 20 kW ×~4, windmill 15 kWextractor 25, smelter 50, workshop 60 kWSCARCE — ≤~100 kW can't cover the iron→steel bootstrap; brownout-throttled. Escape: volcanic colony (geothermal 1 MW) or grind steel. The reachability gate.

EndgameT5–T6, L50+casimir_tap 1 GWmegastructures 60–100 MWRE-TIGHTEN at GW — exotic raws need a fusion array / casimir. Ratios sane (100 MW vs 1 GW).

Feel vs old scale: old values sat within ~10–100× (compressed) → continuous "always need a few more generators." SI makes it punctuated: long scarce early, then cliffs of abundance interrupted by power-hungry-process spikes (EAF/aluminum at T3, megastructures at T5). Brownout keeps scarce phases graceful.

Tuning levers (rates/costs, not power values): early-phase length = steel-chain bootstrap difficulty under brownout (smooth via starter power / a cheaper early generator / volcanic-settlement as an objective); cliff sharpness = gap between best pre-steel source (~20–100 kW) and coal_gen (5 MW) — if too abrupt, add an intermediate steel-free ~500 kW generator; mid/late re-tightens are healthy demand spikes iff the next generator tier is reachable when the hungry consumer unlocks.
05

Balance consequences — tune via rates, not power

  • Generation cliff (accepted): mid-game power cheap once coal_gen unlocked.
  • Coal EROI ≈ 360× (mine 25 kW / 0.9 coal·s⁻¹ vs coal_gen 5 MW / 0.5 coal·s⁻¹) — real ~30–80×. Draws are sound; the ratio is from the mine_on_coal rate. Retune to ~30–80× via mine coal output/rate — a recipe change.
  • EAF / aluminum (10 MW) need ~2× coal_gen or one nuclear/fusion — intentional.
  • Chlor-alkali / ASU / accelerator kept at small-modular end (tunable).
Open (decide in planning): Genesis Chamber draw should scale with the runtime-selected target tier (T1≈50 kW … T4≈50 MW) — a static consumes can't capture it. T3 consumer tier could be higher if T3 should feel more power-hungry.

Trade Offers — a presence-rewarding barter mechanic

Context robot-islands is a keep-it-open idle game whose reward cadence runs on the scale of hours-long tier climbs, leaving nothing to do minute-to-minute. Trade Offers add a short-cadence decision loop: a cheap building periodically surfaces an expiring barter offer biased toward dumping overflow stock for an under-stocked resource. This spec decides the mechanic's rules, valuation, lifecycle, and code boundaries.

Decision
A cheap T1 Signal Exchange building surfaces periodic, online-only, expiring barter offers — biased to dump your fullest stock for your emptiest ever-produced resource, priced at fair per-unit XP-weight value modulated by a tier-distance spread.
The mechanic is a self-correcting inventory pressure-valve (a capped resource stalls its producer and earns zero XP per §9.1; trades relieve that) and a seconds-scale decision loop the game otherwise lacks. Chain-skipping is foreclosed at the root by the ever-produced output filter: you can only receive a resource you already make, so trades reshape the economy you've unlocked rather than shortcutting it.
Deliberately not done: trades grant no XP (progression stays production-only), offers are not persisted (ephemeral, excluded from offline replay), and there is no cross-island or multi-party trading.

01

Background & motivation

What the reader needs to know to evaluate the rest.

The world advances on a live per-frame ticker, so it is visually alive while watched, but its reward cadence is hours-long (tier climbs, milestones). There is no minute-to-minute reason to be present. Trade Offers supply that: an occasional, expiring offer the player judges and accepts.

Two problems are solved at once. First, the cap-stall failure mode — per §9.1 a resource at its storage cap halts its producer and earns zero XP — is relieved by an offer that dumps overflow. Second, offers run online-only, so being present is rewarded while offline parity (§15.5) is untouched: an absent player simply misses optional offers; the base simulation is unchanged.

A per-unit analysis of the live recipe table confirmed the valuation basis: across tier-up recipes the realized XP-weight multiplier is median ≈ 3.20 / tier, matching the nominal §9.1 ladder (1, 3, 10, 30, 100, 300, 1000 → ≈ 3.16×/tier). So pricing a swap at the per-unit weight ratio is fair value; quantity, not per-unit price, is where exploits live — hence the size cap and the ever-produced gate.

02

Why these rules (rejected vs chosen)

The chain-skip exploit and how the design forecloses it.

Rejected — naive value-neutral trades
  • Pricing purely on xp_weight lets a full silo of T0 bulk convert into hundreds of endgame components.
  • A tier-distance cap alone is a blunt restriction unrelated to the player's actual progress.
  • A flat 0.8/tier tax barely dents the ≈3.16×/tier value ramp — far trades stay favorable.
  • Granting XP on swaps turns trading into a leveling printer.
Chosen — ever-produced + per-unit + spread
  • Ever-produced output filter: you can only receive a resource you already make, so trades never shortcut a production chain — and the eligible pool self-scales with progression.
  • Per-unit weight-ratio pricing is fair value, verified against the recipe table.
  • Rolled spread centered on 0.8^Δtier becomes the deal-quality signal — near-tier swaps tend favorable, far-tier tend lossy — making each offer a judgment call that averages ≈ neutral.
  • No XP: progression stays production-only.
03

The offer algorithm

Per Signal-Exchange island, when its spawn timer fires (online time only).

  1. Build pools (per island). Give pool = resources with inv > 0. Get pool = resources in everProduced (regardless of current inventory). Fill% = inv / cap; treat cap == 0 && inv > 0 as ≥ 100% (prime to dump); exclude inv == 0 && cap == 0.
  2. Bias-sample. Give: roll k = 2 from the give pool, keep the higher fill%. Get: roll k = 2 from the get pool, keep the lower fill%. Resample if get == give. Higher k (skill) sharpens the bias.
  3. Tier gate. Reject and resample if Δtier = |tier(get) − tier(give)| exceeds the reach (base 2, skill 3–4). Tier is read from XP_WEIGHT via the §9.1 ladder.
  4. Price. Baseline output-per-input = weight[give] / weight[get] (fair per unit). Multiply by a rolled spread centered on 0.8^Δtier with variance.
  5. Size. giveQty = min(sizePct · inv[give], headroomLimit) where the output is clamped to getQty ≤ cap[get] − inv[get]; whichever side binds, back-compute the other so the swap is exact.
  6. Emit a TradeOffer with spawnedAt / expiresAt.

Acceptance. On accept, re-clamp against current inv[give] and current headroom (production may have moved stock since spawn), then instantly subtract giveQty and add getQty. The offer is consumed. No XP is granted.

Lifecycle. The spawn timer advances only while the tab is focused (visibilityState === 'visible'). One active offer per island (skill may allow a small queue). On accept or expiry the slot frees; the next offer spawns after the cadence. Offers are runtime-only and never persisted.

Surface. A detailed offer card on the active island (if it has a Signal Exchange) plus a global badge — "N offers waiting elsewhere" — so distant offers can be reached before they lapse.

06

Verification

Pure-layer tests in src/trade.test.ts plus a migration test; render layer smoke-tested manually.

Ever-produced gate. generateOffer never outputs a resource absent from everProduced; never gives a resource with inv == 0.
Bias direction. Over many rolls, give skews to high fill% and get skews to low fill% (statistical assertion).
Tier reach. No emitted offer exceeds the configured Δtier; raising reach via effect widens it.
Size + headroom. giveQty ≤ sizePct·stock and getQty ≤ output headroom, with the swap exact at the binding constraint.
Pricing. Output/input ≈ weight[give]/weight[get] × spread; spread center tracks 0.8^Δtier.
Accept semantics. applyOffer shifts inventory exactly, grants zero XP, and re-clamps if stock changed since spawn.
Migration. v18 fixture loads into v19 with everProduced defaulted to inventory keys; v19 round-trips identity.
Budget guard. skilltree-budget.test.ts still passes after adding the node cluster.
Manual. Build a Signal Exchange, fast-forward the spawn timer (test hook), accept an offer, confirm inventory shifts and the badge updates.

08

Out of scope

Explicitly deferred — considered, not missed.

Cross-island / pooled offers
Offers are per-island, sourced from that island's inventory and ever-produced set. A network-wide combined offer is a later idea.
Multi-party / faction trading
No backend exists; offers are generated locally as anonymous barter (scavenger-network fiction). No real counterparties.
Offer persistence across reload
Ephemeral by design — fresh offers regenerate on load, keeping them out of the save/offline-replay path.
Auto-accept / automation
Acceptance is a manual decision — that's the engagement point. Automation would defeat it.
